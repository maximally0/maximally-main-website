# Creating Your First Hackathon

Organizing a hackathon is an exciting way to bring together talented individuals, foster innovation, and create meaningful solutions to real-world problems. This comprehensive guide will walk you through every step of creating a successful hackathon on the Maximally platform.

## Planning Your Hackathon

### Step 1: Define Your Vision

**Choose Your Theme**
Select a theme that is:
- **Relevant**: Addresses current challenges or opportunities
- **Inspiring**: Motivates participants to create innovative solutions
- **Accessible**: Allows for various skill levels and approaches
- **Measurable**: Has clear success criteria and outcomes

**Popular Theme Categories:**
- **Social Impact**: Education, healthcare, environment, poverty
- **Technology Innovation**: AI/ML, blockchain, IoT, cybersecurity
- **Industry-Specific**: Fintech, edtech, healthtech, agritech
- **Creative**: Gaming, AR/VR, design, multimedia
- **Open Innovation**: No restrictions, pure creativity

**Theme Categories:**
- "AI for [Specific Domain] Solutions"
- "Fintech for [Target Audience]"
- "EdTech for Rural Education"
- "Healthcare Accessibility in India"
- "Smart City Solutions"

### Step 2: Set Event Parameters

**Duration Options:**
- **24 Hours**: Intense, focused sprint (Friday evening to Saturday evening)
- **48 Hours**: Most popular format (Friday evening to Sunday evening)
- **Weekend**: Saturday morning to Sunday evening
- **Week-long**: Extended format with daily check-ins
- **Multi-phase**: Idea submission, development, and final presentation phases

**Format Selection:**
- **Online**: Fully virtual with digital collaboration tools
- **Hybrid**: Mix of online and in-person elements
- **In-Person**: Traditional hackathon at a physical venue
- **Distributed**: Multiple locations connected virtually

**Participant Limits:**
- **Small**: 50-100 participants (intimate, high mentorship)
- **Medium**: 100-300 participants (balanced scale and resources)
- **Large**: 300-500 participants (high visibility, competitive)
- **Mega**: 500+ participants (requires significant resources)

## Setting Up Your Event on Maximally

### Event Creation Wizard

**Basic Information:**
- **Event Name**: Clear, memorable, and descriptive
- **Tagline**: One-sentence description of the event's purpose
- **Theme**: Detailed explanation of the challenge or focus area
- **Date & Time**: Start and end times with timezone
- **Format**: Online, hybrid, or in-person
- **Registration Deadline**: When registration closes

**Event Description:**
Write a compelling description that includes:
- **Problem Statement**: What challenge are participants solving?
- **Goals & Objectives**: What do you hope to achieve?
- **Target Audience**: Who should participate?
- **Expected Outcomes**: What kind of solutions are you looking for?
- **Learning Opportunities**: What will participants gain?

**Registration Settings:**
- **Participant Limit**: Maximum number of participants
- **Team Size**: Minimum and maximum team members
- **Eligibility Criteria**: Age, location, skill level requirements
- **Registration Questions**: Custom questions for participants
- **Approval Process**: Automatic or manual approval

### Judging Configuration

**Judging Criteria Setup:**
Define clear, weighted criteria such as:
- **Innovation & Creativity** (25%): Originality and uniqueness of the solution
- **Technical Implementation** (25%): Quality of code and technical execution
- **User Experience** (20%): Design, usability, and user interface
- **Business Viability** (15%): Market potential and sustainability
- **Presentation** (15%): Communication and demo effectiveness

**Judge Recruitment:**
- **Industry Experts**: Senior developers, CTOs, tech leads
- **Entrepreneurs**: Startup founders and business leaders
- **Academics**: Professors and researchers in relevant fields
- **Investors**: VCs and angel investors (for business-focused events)
- **Previous Winners**: Alumni from successful hackathons

**Judge Onboarding:**
- Send detailed judging guidelines
- Provide access to evaluation platform
- Schedule briefing session before event
- Assign specific tracks or categories
- Set expectations for time commitment

### Mentor Network

**Mentor Recruitment:**
- **Technical Mentors**: Help with coding, architecture, and implementation
- **Business Mentors**: Assist with strategy, market analysis, and pitching
- **Design Mentors**: Support UI/UX, branding, and user experience
- **Domain Experts**: Provide specialized knowledge in the theme area

**Mentor Scheduling:**
- **Office Hours**: Scheduled availability for one-on-one help
- **Workshops**: Group sessions on specific topics
- **Roaming Support**: Mentors available for impromptu questions
- **Final Review**: Pre-submission feedback and guidance

## During the Hackathon

### Opening Ceremony

**Agenda (60-90 minutes):**
1. **Welcome & Introductions** (15 minutes)
   - Organizer welcome
   - Platform overview
   - Event schedule

2. **Theme Deep Dive** (20 minutes)
   - Problem statement explanation
   - Success story examples
   - Available resources and APIs

3. **Rules & Guidelines** (15 minutes)
   - Submission requirements
   - Judging criteria
   - Code of conduct
   - Timeline and deadlines

4. **Judge & Mentor Introductions** (15 minutes)
   - Judge backgrounds and expertise
   - Mentor availability and specialties
   - How to access help and support

5. **Team Formation** (15-30 minutes)
   - Team formation process
   - Networking time
   - Platform tutorial

6. **Q&A & Kickoff** (10 minutes)
   - Final questions
   - Official start announcement
   - First milestone reminder

## Judging Process

### Pre-Judging Preparation

**Judge Briefing:**
- **Criteria Review**: Detailed explanation of judging rubric
- **Platform Training**: How to use the evaluation system
- **Timeline Expectations**: When judging begins and ends
- **Calibration Session**: Practice evaluation with sample projects

**Submission Organization:**
- **Category Assignment**: Distribute projects among judges
- **Conflict of Interest**: Identify and manage potential conflicts
- **Evaluation Schedule**: Stagger judging to avoid rush
- **Technical Setup**: Ensure judges can access all submissions

### Evaluation Process

**Initial Screening:**
- **Completeness Check**: Verify all required materials are submitted
- **Technical Functionality**: Confirm projects work as described
- **Eligibility Verification**: Ensure compliance with rules
- **Category Assignment**: Sort projects into appropriate tracks

**Detailed Evaluation:**
- **Individual Scoring**: Each judge evaluates independently
- **Standardized Rubric**: Consistent criteria across all judges
- **Detailed Feedback**: Written comments for each project
- **Score Normalization**: Adjust for judge scoring tendencies

**Consensus Building:**
- **Judge Discussions**: Collaborative review of top projects
- **Tie-Breaking**: Clear process for close decisions
- **Final Rankings**: Agreed-upon winners in each category
- **Feedback Compilation**: Comprehensive feedback for participants

### Results & Recognition

**Winner Selection:**
- **Transparent Process**: Clear explanation of how winners were chosen
- **Multiple Categories**: Recognize different types of excellence
- **Honorable Mentions**: Acknowledge outstanding efforts beyond winners
- **Special Recognition**: Unique achievements and innovations

**Results Announcement:**
- **Closing Ceremony**: Formal presentation of results
- **Live Streaming**: Broadcast for remote participants
- **Social Media**: Real-time updates and winner spotlights
- **Press Release**: Share results with media and partners

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### Low Registration Numbers

**Causes:**
- Insufficient marketing reach
- Unclear value proposition
- Competition with other events
- Limited target audience appeal

**Solutions:**
- **Extended Marketing**: Increase promotion timeline and channels
- **Incentive Programs**: Add early bird bonuses or referral rewards
- **Partnership Expansion**: Collaborate with more organizations
- **Value Communication**: Clarify benefits and learning opportunities

### Technical Difficulties

**Common Issues:**
- Platform crashes or slowdowns
- Submission system failures
- Communication tool problems
- Integration difficulties

**Prevention:**
- **Load Testing**: Test platform capacity before event
- **Backup Systems**: Prepare alternative submission methods
- **Technical Team**: Have dedicated technical support available
- **Clear Instructions**: Provide detailed technical guidelines

### Judge Availability Issues

**Challenges:**
- Judge cancellations
- Inconsistent evaluation standards
- Time zone coordination
- Overwhelming submission volume

**Solutions:**
- **Judge Pool**: Recruit more judges than needed
- **Standardization**: Provide detailed rubrics and training
- **Flexible Scheduling**: Accommodate different time zones
- **Evaluation Tools**: Use platform features to streamline judging

### Participant Engagement Drops

**Warning Signs:**
- Decreased platform activity
- Fewer team formations
- Reduced mentor interactions
- Low submission rates

**Interventions:**
- **Check-in Campaigns**: Proactive outreach to inactive participants
- **Motivation Boosters**: Share inspiring stories and progress updates
- **Additional Support**: Increase mentor availability and workshops
- **Deadline Reminders**: Clear communication about remaining time
