# Maximally — About Page Redesign Task Brief

---

## What's Wrong With the Current Page

The current About page reads like a feature directory — it lists *what exists* (platform, federation, community) without landing the *why this exists* punch. The brand positioning doc is explicit: the About page is not a founding story or a product overview. It's a **positioning statement**. It should answer one question: **why does this need to exist?**

Specific issues to fix:
- "Hackathons as culture, not just code" framing is too soft — it sounds like a thought piece, not a category-defining claim
- The 5-card "What We're Building" grid belongs on the homepage or a products page, not About
- The partners section is fine but misplaced — it's social proof, not positioning
- Nothing on the page communicates the *bar*. Who's in this ecosystem? What does it take to belong?
- Rishul's presence is absent entirely — About pages that say nothing about the founder feel anonymous

---

## New Page Architecture

### Section 1 — Opening Statement (above the fold)
**Not a hero. A declaration.**

Headline: `The world's most serious builder ecosystem.`
Subtext: 1–2 sentences on *why* it needed to be built. The builder ecosystem globally is full of noise. Hackathons with thousands of participants and mediocre output. Serious builders — the ones actually building things that matter — have nowhere to belong. Maximally is that place.

No image. No animation. Just the words. Dark background, the words do the work.

---

### Section 2 — What We Believe (The Manifesto Block)
**2–3 short, punchy paragraphs. Opinionated. No bullets.**

Pull from the positioning doc's core arguments:
- Most platforms compete on size. Maximally competes on signal.
- "Serious" is the entire brand. It defines who belongs, what we build, who we work with.
- Hackathons deserve better. Builders deserve better. We're building the infrastructure that makes that real.

Tone: authoritative, warm, not arrogant. Short sentences. Trust the reader.

---

### Section 3 — The Three Layers (What Maximally Actually Is)
**Clean, minimal 3-block layout. Not cards — just clearly separated content.**

Each layer gets:
- A 1-line name (`The Ecosystem`, `The Enterprise Layer`, `The Media Layer`)
- 2–3 sentences of plain-English explanation
- Who it's for

No icons needed. The words should be enough. If there's a visual element, make it typographic — large numerals `01`, `02`, `03`.

---

### Section 4 — The Bar (Who's In This Ecosystem)
**This is the section that doesn't exist yet and absolutely needs to.**

Current page has no signal about the *quality* of who's inside Maximally. This section fixes that.

Content:
- Mention of the Senior Council — a directory of operators with documented extraordinary ability
- Brief framing of what MFHOP is (50+ vetted organizers, not an open network)
- A stat strip: `12 hackathons executed. 50+ vetted organizers. EB1A and O1 caliber judges. Real execution, not projections.`

This section should feel exclusive, not open. The message: *you have to earn your place here.*

---

### Section 5 — The Founder
**Short. Led by what he's built, not who he is.**

Don't lead with age. Don't make it a personal story. Frame it as: here's the person who understands the builder ecosystem deeply enough to build this, and here's what that understanding is based on.

~3–4 sentences. A real photo (actual, not stock).

---

### Section 6 — The Two CTAs
**Two audiences, two doors.**

- Builders: `Apply to Join →`
- Companies: `Partner With Us →`

Clean. No filler copy around them. Let the page do the convincing; the CTAs just open the door.

---

## Copy Principles for the Writer

Follow the voice from the positioning doc exactly:

**Use:**
- "Serious builders" / "extraordinary operators"
- "High-signal" / "curated ecosystem"
- "Built, not participated"
- "Evidence, not noise"

**Never use:**
- "Super excited to announce..."
- "We're exploring..."
- "Community" without specificity
- "Young founders" or any age-forward framing
- Excessive exclamation points

**Tone:** Authoritative, not arrogant. Precise, not dense. Warm the way serious people are warm — genuine, direct, no corporate padding.

## What to Remove From the Current Page

| Element | Why |
|---|---|
| "Hackathons (Events)" card grid | Belongs on homepage or a programs page |
| Partners section | Move to homepage or a dedicated partners page |
| "Get in Touch" section | Footer handles this |
| Generic subheadlines like "Hackathons as culture, not just code" | Too soft, undercuts the brand |

---

## Design Direction

Stay in the existing visual language (dark bg, orange accent, mono/tight typography) but make this page **sparser and more confident**. Less grid, more white space. The current page is too busy for what an About page needs to do.

- **No stock imagery.** Real photos or no photos.
- **No feature cards.** This isn't a product spec page.
- **Typography does the heavy lifting.** Large, bold, few words per block.
- Stat numbers (12, 50+, etc.) should be displayed large — they're credibility anchors.

---

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Page answers "why does Maximally exist?" within the first two scrolls
- [ ] The bar for membership is communicated (not everyone gets in)
- [ ] Three layers are explained in plain English
- [ ] Senior Council / EB1A angle is present but framed as a quality signal, not a service
- [ ] Founder section is present, led by credibility not bio facts
- [ ] Two clear CTAs (builder + company)
- [ ] No language that signals uncertainty, student focus, or feature-listing
- [ ] Copy reviewed against positioning doc voice guidelines before publish
