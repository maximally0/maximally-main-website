# Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about using the Maximally platform.

## General Questions

### What is Maximally?

Maximally is India's premier hackathon platform that connects students, organizers, and industry professionals. We host high-energy, skill-focused hackathons designed to help participants build real projects, learn cutting-edge technologies, and connect with the tech community.

### Who can participate in Maximally hackathons?

**Students**: High school and college students (ages 16+) from any field
**Professionals**: Developers, designers, and entrepreneurs looking to build or mentor
**Teams**: Groups of 2-5 people with complementary skills
**Beginners**: Many of our hackathons are beginner-friendly with mentorship

### How much does it cost to participate?

**Participation is completely FREE!** This includes:
- Registration for all hackathons
- Access to mentors and judges
- Platform tools and resources
- Certificates upon completion
- Networking opportunities

### What do I need to participate?

**Technical Requirements:**
- Laptop/computer with internet connection
- Basic programming knowledge (varies by event)
- GitHub account (recommended)
- Discord for team communication

**No Experience Required:**
- Many hackathons welcome beginners
- Mentors available to help throughout
- Learning resources provided
- Focus on learning, not just winning

## Hackathon Participation

### How do I find hackathons to join?

1. **Browse Events**: Visit [maximally.in/makeathon](https://maximally.in/makeathon)
2. **Filter by Interest**: Use tags like "AI", "Web Dev", "Mobile", "Beginner-Friendly"
3. **Check Dates**: Find events that fit your schedule
4. **Read Requirements**: Ensure you meet any prerequisites
5. **Register**: Click "Join Hackathon" and complete registration

### Can I participate if I don't know how to code?

**Absolutely!** Many roles don't require coding:
- **UI/UX Design**: Create user interfaces and experiences
- **Business Strategy**: Develop business models and market analysis
- **Content Creation**: Write copy, create presentations, marketing materials
- **Research**: Gather data, analyze problems, validate solutions
- **Project Management**: Coordinate team activities and timelines

### How does team formation work?

**Option 1: Join as Individual**
- Register solo and we'll help you find teammates
- Browse other participants' profiles
- Join our Discord for team formation channels

**Option 2: Create a Team**
- Register and create a team
- Invite friends via email or share team link
- Set team preferences and requirements

**Option 3: Join Existing Team**
- Browse available teams looking for members
- Apply to join teams that match your skills
- Get accepted by team leaders

### What happens during a hackathon?

**Typical 48-Hour Schedule:**

**Friday Evening (6 PM - 10 PM):**
- Opening ceremony and theme announcement
- Team formation and networking
- Mentor introductions
- Initial brainstorming

**Saturday (Full Day):**
- Intensive building and development
- Mentor office hours (every 2 hours)
- Technical workshops and mini-sessions
- Progress check-ins

**Sunday (Morning - 6 PM):**
- Final development push
- Project submission (usually by 2 PM)
- Demo preparation
- Presentations to judges (3 PM - 5 PM)
- Results and closing ceremony (5 PM - 6 PM)

### What should I submit?

**Required Submissions:**
- **Working Demo**: Live version or video demonstration
- **Source Code**: GitHub repository with clear README
- **Presentation**: 3-5 minute pitch deck
- **Documentation**: How to run/use your project

**Optional but Recommended:**
- **Video Demo**: 2-minute walkthrough of your project
- **Technical Architecture**: System design and tech stack
- **Business Plan**: Market analysis and monetization strategy
- **Future Roadmap**: Next steps and improvements

## Judging and Results

### How are projects judged?

**Standard Judging Criteria (varies by event):**

**Innovation & Creativity (25%)**
- Originality of the idea
- Creative problem-solving approach
- Unique use of technology

**Technical Implementation (25%)**
- Code quality and architecture
- Proper use of technologies
- Scalability and performance

**User Experience (20%)**
- Interface design and usability
- User journey and accessibility
- Overall user satisfaction

**Business Viability (15%)**
- Market potential and demand
- Revenue model and sustainability
- Go-to-market strategy

**Presentation (15%)**
- Clarity of communication
- Demo effectiveness
- Team coordination

### When are results announced?

**Timeline varies by event:**
- **Small hackathons (< 50 teams)**: Same day, usually 2-3 hours after submissions
- **Large hackathons (50+ teams)**: Within 24-48 hours
- **Multi-track events**: May take up to a week for comprehensive evaluation

**You'll be notified via:**
- Email to registered address
- Platform notifications
- Discord announcements
- Results page on the event website

## Technical Support

### What if I face technical issues during the hackathon?

**24/7 Support Available:**
- **Discord Help Channel**: Real-time community support
- **Mentor Office Hours**: Scheduled 1-on-1 help sessions
- **Technical Support Team**: Platform and infrastructure issues
- **Emergency Hotline**: Critical issues during submission deadlines

**Common Issues & Solutions:**
- **Submission Problems**: Contact support immediately, we extend deadlines if needed
- **Team Access Issues**: Check team settings and member permissions
- **Platform Bugs**: Report via Discord, we fix critical issues within hours
- **Code Repository Issues**: GitHub integration problems resolved quickly

### Can I use external APIs and services?

**Allowed:**
- **Free APIs**: Google Maps, OpenAI, weather APIs, etc.
- **Free Tiers**: AWS, Google Cloud, Azure free credits
- **Open Source Libraries**: Any MIT, Apache, or similar licenses
- **Public Datasets**: Kaggle, government data, research datasets

**Not Allowed:**
- **Paid Services**: Unless provided by sponsors
- **Pre-built Solutions**: Existing commercial products
- **Copyrighted Content**: Without proper licensing
- **Plagiarized Code**: Must be original work during hackathon

### What programming languages and frameworks can I use?

**No Restrictions!** Use whatever you're comfortable with:

**Popular Choices:**
- **Frontend**: React, Vue, Angular, HTML/CSS/JS
- **Backend**: Node.js, Python (Django/Flask), Java (Spring), PHP
- **Mobile**: React Native, Flutter, Swift, Kotlin
- **AI/ML**: Python (TensorFlow, PyTorch), R, Jupyter notebooks
- **Database**: MongoDB, PostgreSQL, MySQL, Firebase
- **Cloud**: AWS, Google Cloud, Azure, Vercel, Netlify

## Troubleshooting

### I can't log into my account

**Common Solutions:**
1. **Password Reset**: Use "Forgot Password" link
2. **Email Verification**: Check spam folder for verification email
3. **Browser Issues**: Clear cache/cookies or try incognito mode
4. **Account Suspension**: Contact support if account was suspended

**Still Having Issues?**
- Email: [support@maximally.in](mailto:support@maximally.in)
- Discord: @MaximallySupport
- Response time: Usually within 2 hours

### My team member isn't responding

**Steps to Take:**
1. **Try Multiple Channels**: Email, Discord, phone if available
2. **Set Deadline**: Give 24-hour notice for response
3. **Contact Organizers**: We can help mediate or find replacement
4. **Team Adjustment**: Remove inactive members if necessary

**Prevention Tips:**
- Set communication expectations upfront
- Exchange multiple contact methods
- Establish regular check-in times
- Have backup plans for key roles

### I missed the submission deadline

**Immediate Actions:**
1. **Contact Organizers**: Explain situation immediately
2. **Provide Evidence**: Show work completed by deadline
3. **Submit Anyway**: Upload to platform even if late
4. **Request Extension**: Valid reasons may get consideration

**Valid Reasons for Extensions:**
- Technical platform issues
- Medical emergencies
- Natural disasters or power outages
- Critical bugs in submission system
